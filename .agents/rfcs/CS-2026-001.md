# CS-2026-001

## EDAOS: From AI Skill Collection to Evidence-Governed Capability Operating Layer

---

### 1. The AI Capability Governance Problem
As Large Language Models evolved, the industry rushed to equip them with "skills" (search the web, run terminal commands, write to databases). However, the equation became: `More Skills -> More Autonomous Capability -> Less Governance`. 

When an AI system is given raw access to hundreds of unconstrained skills, it behaves unpredictably. It dictates its own logic, ignores institutional guardrails, and bypasses architectural boundaries. The result is a chaotic "Agent" that is impossible to audit and dangerous to deploy in enterprise environments.

### 2. Why Skill Marketplaces Fail
Traditional AI Skill Marketplaces follow a flawed pattern:
`Skill -> Action -> Result`

This model is missing four critical enterprise components:
1. **Provenance**: No cryptographic trace of *why* an action was taken.
2. **Permission Boundary**: Skills often operate without standardized governance boundaries, making permission scope, provenance, and accountability difficult to enforce.
3. **Evidence Integrity**: Outputs are volatile strings rather than normalized, hash-verified data structures.
4. **Conflict Resolution**: When two skills disagree, the system arbitrarily picks one (usually the one with the highest LLM confidence score) rather than recognizing the tension.

### 3. EDAOS Design Thesis
The core principle of EDAOS (Evidence-Governed AI Operating Layer):
> *AI capability should not directly execute intelligence. It should contribute evidence into a governed decision system.*

### 4. Architecture: Evidence-Governed AI Operating Layer
EDAOS is not a traditional operating system (lacking traditional resource scheduling or hardware abstraction). It is an **Operating Layer** that governs AI capabilities. It strictly isolates the pipeline:
1. **Capability Marketplace**: External intelligence is mapped to strict YAML contracts.
2. **Evidence Layer**: Capabilities generate cryptographically hashed Evidence (not actions).
3. **Decision Layer**: The Runtime evaluates Evidence and binds it to a Decision Contract.
4. **Governed Execution Runtime**: Executes the finalized Decision Plan through a formalized Adapter Contract.

### 5. Capability Lifecycle
Capabilities do not exist statically; they follow a strict governance lifecycle:
```text
                    Registration
                         |
                         v
External Capability → Validation → Certification
                         |
                         v
                  Evidence Production
                         |
                         v
                    Decision Layer
                         |
                         v
                    Execution
                         |
                         v
                    Audit Record
```

### 6. Golden Case: Last30Days
To prove the architecture, we implemented the `market-research-last30days` capability.
Instead of allowing the capability to research Reddit and immediately publish a marketing strategy, it is governed by the EDAOS pipeline:
```text
Social Signal
      |
Observation
      |
Evidence Bundle
      |
Decision Record
      |
Governed Execution Plan
      |
Feature Strategy
```
The capability only supplies the Evidence. The Runtime dictates the Strategy.

### 7. Architectural Invariant Guarantees
The stability of this Operating Layer is not proven by unit tests, but by 5 executable Architectural Invariant Guarantees:

| Invariant | Guarantees |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hash Verification** | Evidence Identity |
| **Malicious Rejection** | Capability Isolation |
| **Evidence Immutability** | Historical Integrity |
| **Semantic Drift Protection** | Contract Stability |
| **Decision Tension** | Decision Transparency |

### 8. Enterprise Implications
The deployment of EDAOS demonstrates a fundamental shift for organizations leveraging AI agents:
> *Governance is not a limitation on AI autonomy; it is the infrastructure that allows autonomy to operate safely at scale.*

By rejecting the "AI Skill Chaos" paradigm, enterprises can finally trust the provenance and predictability of multi-agent intelligence.

### 9. Lessons Learned
The most profound lesson from building EDAOS v1.0 is that:
> *The future of AI systems is not collecting more skills. It is governing more capabilities.*
